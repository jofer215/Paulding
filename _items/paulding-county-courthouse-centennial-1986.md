A centennial history of Paulding County's county seats and courthouses,
researched and written by Melinda Huseby Krick for the Courthouse
Centennial celebration held June 20-21, 1986 — 100 years after the
current courthouse's cornerstone was laid. Covers the same county-seat
succession (New Rochester, 1839-1841; Charloe, 1841-1850; Paulding,
since 1850) documented elsewhere in this archive, including in the
[Sheriff's Office history]({{ "/archive/history-of-paulding-county-sheriffs-office/" | relative_url }})
and the
[unattributed county history typescript]({{ "/archive/paulding-county-early-history-typescript/" | relative_url }}).

The centennial program included a Friday-evening bandstand dedication
and Bavarian Brass concert, and a Saturday parade, courthouse-square
festivities, and a cornerstone-opening ceremony.

See the full PDF for the complete history and centennial program.
