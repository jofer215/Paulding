The Wabash & Erie Canal and the Miami Canal were built through Paulding
County in the mid-1840s, converging at the village of Junction. For a
period, that convergence made Junction a genuine rival to Fort Wayne as a
regional center of commerce — goods, laborers, and merchants moved through
the county because of infrastructure dug largely by hand by canal
laborers, many of them recent immigrants working under harsh and often
dangerous conditions.

The canals' economic significance is usually credited to the towns that
benefited from them, but the underlying story is the workforce that built
and maintained them. Remnants of both canal systems are still visible in
the county today. If you have records of canal labor camps, company
ledgers, immigrant labor accounts, or local news coverage of the canal
era, they would substantially deepen this entry beyond the summary given
here.
