A pictorial history of Charloe — Paulding County's second county seat
(1841-1850, after New Rochester and before Paulding) — dedicated "in
memory of the last Indian chief in northwestern Ohio," Charloe Peter,
for whom the village was named. See this archive's
[unattributed county history typescript]({{ "/archive/paulding-county-early-history-typescript/" | relative_url }})
and
[Indigenous Peoples of Paulding County]({{ "/archive/indigenous-peoples-of-paulding-county/" | relative_url }})
for more on Charloe Peter and the Oquanoxas reservation.

The booklet also reproduces the program from a **historical marker
dedication ceremony held May 27, 1962**, at 2:00 p.m. — presentation of
the marker by Otto E. Ehrhart of Antwerp, greetings from Carson Eakins
of Charloe, a poem titled "Village By A Stream" by Douglas Mead, and a
dedicatory address by U.S. Rep. Delbert L. Latta (5th District). This
is very likely the original dedication of one of the undated Charloe
markers in this archive's
[historical markers index]({{ "/archive/historical-markers-index/" | relative_url }})
— probably "Charloe (Oquanoxa's Village, on the Auglaize River)," given
the matching poem title — though which specific marker isn't confirmed.

See the full PDF for the complete pictorial history and program.
