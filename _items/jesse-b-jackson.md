*By Kim Sutton, Paulding County History Museum*

Jesse B. Jackson was born in Paulding, Ohio in 1871. In 1908, he was
appointed as the American Consul in Aleppo, Syria. He is credited with
saving the lives of thousands of Armenian people in the Armenian
Genocide that took place during and after WWI.

The Paulding County History Museum receives phone calls and inquiries
about Paulding County history and the people who lived here quite often.
One such phone call was received recently from California that was
quite unique. The call was from Mr. Seth Ourfalian of Westminster,
California. Mr. Ourfalian is retired from the computer science industry
and became interested in history, traveling around the world studying
various subjects. He is 100% Armenian but has been living in the United
States since the 1980s.

If you are of Armenian descent, you know about Jesse Jackson. No, not
the political activist who recently passed away, but the legendary Jesse
B. Jackson from Paulding, Ohio who was appointed as consul to Aleppo,
Syria in 1908. Jesse B. Jackson is credited with saving thousands of
Armenian people from the genocide that was taking place during and after
WWI.

Mr. Ourfalian found us through his investigation into the life of Jesse
B. Jackson. He was able to locate Jesse's great grandson, Jerett
Jackson, in Dallas, Texas. Jerett told Mr. Ourfalian that his family had
disposed of all the artifacts from Jesse's estate and given everything
to an organization that was going to start an Armenian Genocide Museum
in Washington, D.C. Apparently, a lawsuit was filed against the group as
there is some disagreement as to whether a genocide was actually taking
place. The Turkish government has held the position that no genocide was
planned nor happened and that is still their position today.

The museum was never built, and Mr. Jerett Jackson does not know what
happened to all the artifacts they had donated. He did know, however,
that there were a lot of other items that had been given to Jesse's
family descendants living in Columbus, Ohio. Jerett gave the contact
info to Mr. Ourfalian, who gave them a call.

Yes! They had two tubs of letters, pictures, Armenian clothes and other
items, but they had given it to three ladies from the Paulding County
History Museum. They felt it should all go back to where Jesse came
from, so they contacted our museum. Melinda Krick, Jane Nice and I made
a trip to Columbus to bring it all back. Mr. Marcus Shera, research
assistant and nephew of Seth Ourfalian, stopped in Paulding on his way
from California to Philadelphia to start a new job to document all the
letters, papers, photographs, etc. held by our museum. I met with Marcus
for two days at the museum so he could complete his work.

We are now currently in the process of archiving the historic documents
and other artifacts we brought back with us and will have some of them
on display at the museum during the Spring Tea.

As it turns out, I am related to Jesse B. Jackson. His mother was Lucy
Brown. My great-great-grandfather was George Brown, Lucy's brother! Our
common great-great-great-grandfather was Elijah J. Brown, one of the
first doctors in Paulding County.
