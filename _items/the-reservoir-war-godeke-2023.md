A 445-page history of the "Reservoir War" — the night of April 25,
1887, when roughly 200 men calling themselves "The Dynamiters," under a
banner reading "No Compromise! The Reservoir Must Go!," destroyed the
Wabash and Erie Canal's Six Mile Reservoir east of Antwerp: overpowering
its guards, dynamiting the bulkhead and lock, and burning the
lockkeeper's house. The reservoir, built in 1840, had become mostly a
breeding ground for malaria-carrying mosquitoes once canal traffic
dried up in the 1870s; state legislative attempts to abandon it had
repeatedly failed. The Dynamiters were never identified or punished,
and the reservoir's abandonment became law the following year. The
county's motto, "No Compromise," comes from this event.
