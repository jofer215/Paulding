Before it was Paulding County, this land carried a string of larger
claims: the French called northwestern Ohio "the Black Swamp" and
grouped it into "New France"; the English crown's colonial charters
stretched some of the original colonies' claims across it too (putting
the area nominally in both Connecticut and Virginia at different
points); after the Northwest Ordinance it became part of the Northwest
Territory, then Wayne County (Ohio's first, covering 26 later
counties), then Defiance County territory administratively from
1824-1839.

Paulding County organized its own government in 1839, with county
offices at New Rochester on the Maumee — "the largest village in the
county at that time." The seat moved to Charloe in 1841 ("on the
promise of a big new courthouse"), then to Paulding in 1855, "largely
because it was more centrally located." In 1845 Williams County's seat
moved to Bryan, and Paulding County gave up half of Auglaize Township
(18 sections) to help settle unrest there, receiving four sections of
Emerald Township from Defiance County in exchange — leaving ten of the
county's twelve townships at the standard 36 sections.
