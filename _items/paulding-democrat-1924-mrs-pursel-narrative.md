Mrs. A.M. Pursel came to Paulding as a five- or six-year-old girl in
1850 — "when the county seat was moved from Charloe to this place" —
traveling by the old Anthony Wayne and Fort Defiance road, the only
wagon road between those points. She recalled only five families
living there at the time (Campbells, Torrys, Hickersons, Kohns, and
Shafers from Indiana, plus Judge Latty from Charloe), abundant wild
game (deer, squirrel, coon, and occasional bear, with wolves howling
at night), and the first term of court held in her family's own home —
the first house built in Paulding — with Judge Latty presiding. She
also recalled the first courthouse, a two-story frame building that
later burned.
