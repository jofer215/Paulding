A three-part compilation of early local history:

**"Brief History of the Old M.E. Church, and Its Members"** — Dr. John
Lincoln's history of Methodism in Antwerp and vicinity, originally
printed in the Argus, Sept. 7, 1899. Lincoln, who came to Antwerp in
1842, recounts the circuit's earliest appointments (at William Banks'
farm and the widow Murphy's, both outside Antwerp), a list of the
circuit-riding preachers from 1842 through 1871, the first society
organized in Antwerp in November 1849 (ten members, including Lincoln
and his wife), the 1855 division of the old Defiance Mission into the
new Paulding Mission, and the 1859-60 construction of the first church
building — funded partly by a $90 donation from the Ladies' Sewing
Society and built under contract by A.H. Boland for $600.

**"Pioneer Letter — T.W. Lincoln, 1907"** — a letter from T.W. Lincoln,
then living in Long Beach, California, to the editor of the Bee Argus,
recalling his father's 1844 diary of early Carryall Township settlement
along the Maumee — his parents' marriage (Nov. 20, 1844), building a
16x20-foot round-log cabin, and a roster of the township's first
settlers.

**"Early History of Paulding and Paulding County"** — a short,
unattributed summary covering George Marsh's 1850 platting of the
village of Paulding, its 1873 incorporation, the county's 1820
formation from Northwestern Ohio Indian lands, the county-seat
succession (New Rochester, 1839; Charloe, 1841; Paulding, 1852), and
John Paulding's role as one of Major André's captors. This closely
tracks material already covered in this archive's
[unattributed county history typescript]({{ "/archive/paulding-county-early-history-typescript/" | relative_url }})
and the
[county engineer's overview]({{ "/archive/paulding-county-history-engineer-overview/" | relative_url }}).
