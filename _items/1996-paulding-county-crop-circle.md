In July 1996, a crop circle was discovered a few miles outside Paulding,
becoming the talk of the county and drawing regional and multi-state
attention amid the broader 1990s wave of public fascination with crop
circles, UFOs, and paranormal phenomena.

The event was covered in a series of articles in *The Crescent-News*,
reproduced here from newspaper clippings:

- ["Was It Work of Aliens or a Hoax?"](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/was-it-work-of-aliens-or-a-hoax/) — Jim Langham, July 1996
- ["Jury Is Still Out."](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/jury-is-still-out/) — Staff and wire reports, July 1996
- ["Paulding Family Deals with 'Circle-Mania.'"](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/paulding-family-deals-with-circle-mania/) — Jim Langham, July 14, 1996
- ["Crop Circle T-Shirts on Sale, Proceeds Will Go to Hospital."](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/crop-circle-t-shirts-on-sale-proceeds-will-go-to-hospital/) — Jim Langham, July 1996
- ["Family Attempts to Be Fair and Generous to the Public."](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/family-attempts-to-be-fair-and-generous-to-the-public/) — Jim Langham, July 1996
- ["'Significant Change' Seen in Wheat at Circle Site."](https://pauldinghistory.wordpress.com/2020/08/02/significant-change-seen-in-wheat-at-circle-site/) — Jim Langham, July 1996

kind of local-news episode that rarely survives outside personal
