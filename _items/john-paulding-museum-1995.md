A feature on the John Paulding Historical Society Museum, founded by
volunteers roughly a decade before this 1995 article — "just a little
more than 10 years ago, there wasn't a historical museum in Paulding
County. In fact, little of the county's history was even collected."

Highlights described: a clothes safe made locally (from Paulding County
timber, since heavier furniture was too costly to haul in from the
East), a hand-woven blanket (linen and wool the maker grew, sheared,
and spun herself), a man's collar box, a trunk carried by the Philips
family fleeing the South via the Underground Railroad, a map pinpointing
one-room schoolhouses (some with names like "Happy Corners" and
"Sunnyside"), and — housed in a separate building — antique tractors,
a 1928 Farmall Regular, threshing machines, a sleigh, and a carriage,
restored by volunteers including museum board member John Stuart.
Museum contacts given: Leo and Caroline Davis, Royce and Ruby Smith.
