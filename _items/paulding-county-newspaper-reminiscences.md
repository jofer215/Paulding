## "Grand Old Pioneer Women!"

*Rev. H. A. Brown, tribute to the "trials and hardships" of early women
settlers, in his address at Pioneer Day at Antwerp, 1888, as published in
the Antwerp Argus newspaper:*

"I would think myself ungrateful if I did not mention that one class of
persons that have borne so important a part of the burden in the
development of the resources of this country. I mean the great hearted,
noble, self-sacrificing, never flagging women! Ready for every
emergency; willing to bear her part of the burden. You pioneers who are
here to-day well remember how many of the women of those days wielded
the ax, picked the brush, helped to log, husked the corn, waded the
swales, reaped and bound the wheat and helped to haul it in, as well as
cared for the sick and entertained strangers, besides raising their own
families of boys and girls, and did their house work and kept their
cabins neat and tidy as pins. And when the time comes that succeeding
generations are building monuments to the memory of the old pioneers who
are dead and gone — as they record their names on those marble shafts,
the topmost inscription, I fancy, that will be read there will be —
'Grand Old Pioneer Women!'"

## Do You Remember —

*From the Paulding County Republican of May 8, 1919:*

When there was a long row of county office buildings in Paulding between
Perry and Jackson, facing on Main Street, with a frame court room at the
extreme north end of the string?

When the first safety bicycle came to town? It took the owner several
days to learn how to run it. Now days any real kid can learn to ride one
of them in 15 minutes.

When the county seat was at New Rochester over on the Maumee River? It
was one of the main stops on the stage line between Toledo and Fort
Wayne and for a number of years enjoyed a good business.

When the county seat was at Charloe and the courthouse was a two story
brick structure?

When four horses couldn't pull an empty wagon through the mud on the
south side of the square in Paulding?

When the first automobile came to town? It created nearly as much
excitement as the first safety bicycle.

When the only road between Charloe and Paulding was under water
three-fifths of the time and the only way to make the trip between the
two places was by [crawling across] logs?

When the biggest showplace in this section of the state was a plank road
that led back into the woods from Holcombville? It was the only way they
could get timber to the mill.
