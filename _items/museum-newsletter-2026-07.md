Opens with a tribute to Elizabeth Miller, a longtime museum volunteer
and former director who chaired the Festival of Trees and the Ladies'
Teas and cared for the museum's vintage clothing collection, who died
June 22 after a battle with lung cancer.

Also covers: the nearly-finished kitchen remodel, dedicated at the
May 31 General Meeting to Les and Sandy Weidenhamer (Les was a former
historical society president); the annual Quilt Show, held during the
Paulding County Fair and drawing about 120 visitors to see 70 quilts;
a new parade float built from a donated flatbed wagon; a CCAO/CEAO
county-leaders meeting hosted at the museum; a recap of the ATHS
Vintage Truck Show's founding in 2000, ahead of its 25th running on
Sept. 20, 2026; plans for the Sept. 19 Antique Tractor Show and an
August "Family Fun Night"; a volunteer call for this year's Festival of
Trees ("A Few of My Favorite Things"); and a list of recent donations,
including a 7-1/2-inch gauge steam locomotive, caboose, and track from
local collector Stan Searing.
